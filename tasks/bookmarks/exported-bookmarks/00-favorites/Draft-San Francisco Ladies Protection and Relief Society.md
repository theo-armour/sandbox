## Excerpt

The San Francisco Ladies Protection and Relief Society, doing business as "Heritage on the Marina", is a non-profit foundation that operates a retirement community in the Marina District of San Francisco, California. Founded in 1853 &#91;1&#93; with the mission "to render protection and assistance to strangers, to sick independent women and children," the organization has provided a variety of philanthropic benefits to generations of San Franciscans.&#91;2&#93; Currently providing four levels of care (independent living, assisted-living, memory-care, and skilled nursing,&#91;3&#93; the current plan envisages transitioning to an aging-in-place model. The current premises are of historical and architectural merit and were designed by architect Julia Morgan in 1923 and completed in 1925.&#91;4&#93;
