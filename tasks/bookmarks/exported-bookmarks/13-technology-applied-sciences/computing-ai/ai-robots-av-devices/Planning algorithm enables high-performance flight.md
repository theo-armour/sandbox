## Excerpt

New algorithms for trajectory planning and control of fixed-wing “tailsitter” aircraft are faster and more efficient than traditional quadcopter drones. The algorithms, developed at MIT, can execute challenging maneuvers and are so efficient they can plan complex trajectories in real-time.
